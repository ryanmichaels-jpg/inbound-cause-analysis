## Campaign Objective

Increase inbound volume from ops-aware B2B buyers who have already quantified their manual workflow pain, by producing content that mirrors the discovery moment podcast listeners describe — hearing a specific problem named and feeling compelled to reach out.

## Core Insight & Angle

The unifying theme across all three buyer quotes is not a feature request — it is a labor cost framing. Buyers are calculating hours lost (9 hours/week, 12 hours/week) and translating that into headcount or opportunity cost ('that's a headcount,' 'more time on admin than on real work'). The content angle is: 'The Manual Ops Tax' — the hidden weekly labor cost of running disconnected sales and marketing tools. This is not a pain we are inventing; it is the exact language buyers used unprompted.

## Target Audience

- Primary: Revenue Ops Managers and Sales Ops leads at growth-stage B2B companies running a CRM alongside a separate sales engagement or list-building tool.
- Secondary: VP of Sales or Head of Marketing who owns headcount budget and feels the downstream effects of ops team bottlenecks.
- Psychographic signal: Buyers who have already done informal time-tracking on manual tasks — they are quantification-oriented and close to a buying decision.

## Key Messages (Grounded in Buyer Language)

- Name the tax explicitly: 9–12 hours per ops person per week spent on copy-paste between tools is a recurring, measurable cost — not an inconvenience.
- Reframe the budget conversation: When ops time is calculated at loaded headcount cost, manual list-building and CRM-to-tool syncing is not a workflow problem, it is a line-item expense.
- Validate the diagnosis: Buyers who have noticed this pattern are not outliers — this is a structural problem created by point-solution stacks, not a process failure by the ops team.

## Content Formats & Prioritization

- 1. PODCAST EPISODE (highest priority): A dedicated episode titled around 'The Manual Ops Tax' — structured as a problem-definition episode, not a product pitch. Use the 9–12 hour/week framing as the hook in the first 90 seconds. This mirrors the exact discovery moment buyers described ('caught your podcast episode and had to reach out').
- 2. COMPANION LONG-FORM ARTICLE: A 1,000–1,500 word piece that walks through how to calculate your own manual ops tax — hours per task × frequency × loaded hourly cost. Designed to be shared internally by ops managers making a business case upward.
- 3. LINKEDIN ORGANIC POST (not paid): A single-stat post framing the 9–12 hour figure as a weekly cost, written in first-person ops-manager voice. Organic only — this audience self-selects; paid amplification to cold audiences has demonstrated a 3.0% close rate versus 30% from earned/organic podcast discovery.
- 4. SALES ONE-PAGER: A leave-behind for AEs to use with ops-persona prospects that opens with the hours-to-headcount calculation framework, not product features.

## What to Avoid

- Do not lead with product capabilities or integration lists — none of the high-converting buyer quotes reference features.
- Do not use vague efficiency language ('streamline,' 'optimize') — buyers in this segment speak in hours and headcount; the content must match that register.
- Do not replicate this campaign on LinkedIn paid without first testing organic engagement — the channel data shows paid LinkedIn significantly underperforms this audience's conversion behavior.
