Inbound Cause Analysis

A root-cause analysis pipeline for inbound GTM — flipped from what to fix to what to amplify.

GTM teams optimize tactics — channel mix, copy tests, routing rules — and still cannot answer the question in the middle: which messages, channels, and journeys actually produced revenue. ICA reconstructs why inbound converts from 2,500 leads of synthetic CRM data stress-tested against realistic CRM noise, then auto-drafts the GTM plays to do more of it.

What it found — five patterns in why inbound converts
F1
Channel-quality surprise. Podcast leads close at 31%; LinkedIn-paid leads at 4.4% — a quality gap that runs directly against volume.
7×
F2
Message–persona resonance. A manual-work-reduction message closes mid-market RevOps leaders at 26% vs 4.7% for every other persona.
5.5×
F3
The winning journey. A podcast → organic-search → demo path within 14 days closes at 39% against an 8.2% baseline.
5×
F4
ICP fit vs volume. The largest LinkedIn campaign carries an 80% bad-outcome share — mean ICP-fit score 37 against a dataset-wide 53.
80%
F5
Compliance resonance. Enterprise IT buyers close at 21% on a compliance/security message — a lift over every other persona.
4×
Synthetic data + v1.5 noise layer → 5 SQL findings → Claude resonance extraction → Claude artifact generation → Streamlit dashboard. The LLM layer reads 2,039 buyer-text snippets (25.5% empty-text filtered as missingness) and re-derives the themes independently under noise — 94.4% agreement with ground truth (clean baseline: 94.1%), 99.2% stable across three runs. Verified against a pre-committed >80% acceptance gate.

Why it matters Most analytics stops at a dashboard: here is what happened, good luck. ICA closes the loop — every finding ends in a GTM artifact a team can ship this week, emitted as Markdown to read and JSON to route into Marketo, HubSpot, or Salesforce. It runs on a deterministic synthetic dataset and is one ingestion swap away from real CRM data. That loop — analysis that ends in shipped action, not another dashboard — is what a GTM Engineer who can build delivers. And every claim above has been re-verified after the dataset was deliberately corrupted with realistic CRM noise.
